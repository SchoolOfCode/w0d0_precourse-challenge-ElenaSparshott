Charles Dowding
A no dig gardening pioneer

Charles is based on Homeacres Farm in South Somerset. The basis of the 'no dig' method is to not dig the garden and disturb the microenviroment but to apply a thick layer of mulch once a year. The mulch is often good quality compost and vegetables are planted directly into the beds with wonderful results. He started growing vegetables in 1981. Now with 40 years experience Charles runs couses, writes books and has a popular youtube channel.

The advantages of no dig, for the soil and the gardener.

  1. Gardens can be started at any time of the year

  2. There is no need to dig or weed first, just cover with a mulch

  3. With compost mulches you can plant vegetables on the same day

  4. Gardening is more enjoyable because less weeds germinate

  5. Mycorrhizal fungi stay intact aiding growth

  6. Moisture is retained

  7. Drainage is very good. Mud does not stick to your boots in wet weather

  8. Resowing and replanting can be done quickly at any time of the year

  9. Warmth is retained by the soil in winter

  10. Carbon stays in the soil

  11. Once established less soil is needed than if the soil was dug
